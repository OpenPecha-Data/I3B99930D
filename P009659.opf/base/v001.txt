Manuscript written in a careful and pleasing bam-yig with the usual
bsdu-yig in abundance. Calligraphed in black and red on a laminated
white machine-made paper without watermark, the manuscript would
seem to be no older than ten years. The hand is certainly Khampa.
The few orthographical variants, e. g. bskul and bskur, suggest
that the scribe was either a very careful copyist or was a speaker
of a Khampa dialect like that of Go-'jo.
Source: B. Jamyang Norbu who confirms that the copy was made in
India from an original belonging to the late Kah-thog Ma-ni Bla-
ma of NEFA. The whereabouts of the original is unknown. Cf. the
manuscript reported by John Weir Hardy (could this be Ma-ni Bla-
ma's original?).
Authorship: 'Dan Bla-ma Chos-kyi-dbang-phyug who concealed his
work at a place in the Mgo-log country; rediscovered by Drag-rtsal-
rdo-rje of Gling-tshang.
